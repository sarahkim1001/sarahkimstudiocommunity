Hello, I'm Sarah!

I am a multidisciplinary practitioner, designer, and cultural researcher dedicated to building spaces, conversations, and digital narratives that bring art communities closer together. Since graduating from New York University with a degree in Integrated Design and Media, I have focused my practice on community-centered storytelling and experience design—deepening engagement within fine art galleries through thoughtful programming and creative digital documentation.

Whether I am documenting the intimate studio methods of artists through cinematography, handling communications for engaging panel dialogues, or architecting digital ecosystems that amplify gallery programming, my goal is to foster deep, intuitive connections between creators and the public.

While my daily practice is rooted in community curation and media strategy, I also maintain an independent, nature-inspired functional art and design practice where I explore contemporary folklore, myth, and organic geometry. You can wander into that side of my world at Sakha Studios →.

Current Explorations

  • Cultural Storytelling: Documenting the personal histories, intuitive workflows, and studio environments of regional creators through fine-art photography, cinematography, and archival research.
  • Community Curation: Designing gatherings, panel dialogues, and on-site gallery experiences that deepen public intimacy with exhibitions and local cultural ecosystems.
  • Media Strategy: Crafting cohesive digital identities across social platforms, campaign design, and web interfaces to extend the narrative reach of community-centered art initiatives.

I am always looking to collaborate with independent creators, studios, and institutions who value intentional space-making and the harmonious systems that bring us closer together. If you are exploring physical-digital engagement or simply want to share a conversation, please reach out.
